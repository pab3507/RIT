About Us

The Latin Rhythm Dance Club is a organization that attracts a variety of students from diverse backgrounds. It is a mix of cultures and identities that come together for their love of Latin dance and music including salsa, bachata and merengue. Our purpose is to engage students in social interaction by dancing while still offering the opportunity to prepare for performances. Being part of the Latin Rhythm Dance Club at RIT will be a way for you to meet new friends, explore your hobby, enhance your talent, and have fun.

Meetings

The Latin Rhythm Dance Club meets every tuesday of both Fall and Spring Semesters to promote latin dance and music! Our weekly meetings consists of lessons, taught by our coordinator of dance instruction, on Salsa, Bachata and Merengue. Ocasionally we will have special guest instructors teach a special lesson on a specific type of dance such as Kizomba, Zouk, Rueda, etc. Our meetings normally are at the Upper Dance Studio on the Student Life Center. We run our meetings from 8PM to 10PM. Everyone is welcome to assist!

Lessons

The Latin Rhythm Dance Club offers free dance lessons on several genres of music, mainly on Salsa and Bachata. Our coordinator of dance instruction is responsible for engaging students throughout the lesson and making sure everyone is included and learning throughout the process. Lessons are always from the beginners perspective, so all levels are welcome. First-timers as well as seasoned pros are invited to participate. On special ocassions, guest dance instructors will teach lessons on certain musical genres such as Kizomba, Rueda, Zouk, etc. Come join us to learn and have fun!!

Salsa

Salsa is danced by stepping on 3 consecutive beats of music and then pausing for 1 beat, then repeating. it includes lots of fun, complete with spins, sharp movement, and crisp turns.

Bachata

Bachata is a very sensual and romantic dance. It is danced all over the world but not identically. The basics to the dance are three-step with a Cuban hip motion, followed by a tap including a hip movement on the 4th beat.

Kizomba

Kizomba is a sensual dance with influences of Tango, but it differs that in that the lower body and hip do forward/backwards and circle motions. People dance on the tempo, as well as playing on the off beat.
